Understanding your racket

Swingweight Explained

Swingweight describes how a racket feels while being swung - combining total weight with how that weight is distributed through the head.

Higher Swingweight

  • Feels heavier but delivers more power
  • Greater stability at ball impact
  • Easier to generate pace and hold control
  • Requires more physical effort to swing
  • Preferred by experienced players

Lower Swingweight

  • Easier and faster to move through the air
  • Better manoeuvrability and topspin potential
  • Demands better technique and timing
  • Less strain - good if you have arm issues
  • Recommended for beginners and juniors
String bed analysis

ERT 300 Computer

The only microprocessor worldwide for automatic measurement of string bed stiffness - used at the world's most prestigious tournaments.

What it does

The Dynamic Tension (DT) value

The ERT 300 measures the actual playing characteristics of your strung racket. A lower DT means a softer, more powerful feel. A higher DT means a firmer, more controlled response. The value is influenced by string type, gauge, tension, machine calibration and stringer technique.

DT Range Category Playing Style
28–34 LO Baseline defensive play - softer feel, more power from the strings
35–41 MID Dynamic offensive approach - balanced power and control
42–46 HI Powerful, aggressive play - firmer bed for precision placement
47–54 HI+ Very fast, aggressive play - maximum control for tour-level hitters
